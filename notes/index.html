Chronological Notes from INFO 461, Offered Spring 2018

March 27, 2018 - Class 1.1

The first class went really well. And the persistence lab was good, for the most part, too.

A few bugs:

  1. I didn't get the time of day right for the due date of the readings on Canvas. That confused the students.
  2. It was weird to say that thing to turn in for the main body of the reading isn't due until I ask a question about it in class, whereas the writeup about the additional reading is due before class. Not bad, just weird.
  3. When I imported your Canvas file, I got only one 2pt assignment for Reading 1.1, rather than 2 1pt assignments (one for the in-class question and one for the additional reading). I left it that way, but realized it was weird last night.
  4. I didn't ask any in-class question for the first reading, since I didn't want to make them do homework before they even met me, but then I didn't want them to answer an unwritten question for what they turned in on Thursday. So I had them write the same summary for the in-class reading as they would for the additional reading.
  5. Sometimes the "additional" reading is referred to as a "selected" reading. That inconsistency of terminology confused the students.
  6. In the next iteration, I'd suggest to eliminate the single point in-class reading writeup for class #1. Just have them do the additional reading writeup due at the beginning of the next class along with the expected writeup of the additional reading for class 1.2.

Some observations about the Persistence lab being first with its javascript text-color changing code task:

  1. Two design-focused students came up to me at the end to ask if coding was a significant part of the project. Could they get away with just doing design and let someone else do the coding?
  2. Two more students worried that since they hadn't done JS programming for at least a year, that they would do poorly in the course. They're worried that programming JS in class without any help is going to prove to be the rule, not an exception.
